Math Tricks & Techniques

Multiply numbers from 11 to 19 in your head

Example: 14 × 17

Step 1: Add outer digit of lower number to upper: \(17 + 4 = 21\) → “210” as the hundreds/tens part.
Step 2: Multiply inner digits: \(4 × 7 = 28\).
Step 3: Add: 210 + 28 = 238.

Square any two‑digit number ending in 5

Example: 75²

Take the first digit (7), multiply by next integer (8): \(7 × 8 = 56\). Then append “25”: result is 5625.

Quadratic Formula

If you have \(ax² + bx + c = 0\), you can find x by:

\[\displaystyle x = \frac{−b \pm \sqrt{b² − 4ac}}{2a}\]

This is a foundational tool you can also explain stepwise to boost mental recall.

Sum of consecutive integers shortcut

Example: Sum numbers from 1 to 100.

Use formula: \(\frac{n(n+1)}{2} = \frac{100 × 101}{2} = 5050\).
